Transgressive movement and its influence on economic & social changes

Consequences of graffiti activity

In the late 1960s, graffiti appeared in Philadelphia. The artists would use inaccessible public places to paint graffiti. However, it quickly became a transgressional movement, that’s why it was considered as vandalism, so some cities decided to fight it.
Galleries started buying some artworks, that’s why it spread out and took different forms. A lot of street art artists are now successful because plenty of people compete to collect their artworks. They have also become drivers who help poor communities.

The graffiti as a revitalizing tool

Modern artists, especially graffiti and street art artists, influence economic and social changes in impoverished neighborhoods because their artworks are used to revitalize these areas, attracting tourists.
There has also been a change in the main competition among artists, especially since the introduction of graffiti, because of the popularity of the street art in the United States at the end of 20th century.
The graffiti was created by teenagers. As a result, the society’s perception toward the artists has changed, making the new style of art possible for all ages, not only for professional adults.
The graffiti paint gained so much acclaim and admiration, that some art galleries started buying it.
