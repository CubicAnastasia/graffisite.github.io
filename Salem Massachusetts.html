Salem Punto Art Museum

What are its goals?

First, Salem Punto Urban Art Museum’s mission is to increase economic opportunities for local businesses and break down socio-economic barriers. It also instills neighborhood pride when street artists share their stories through their murals and provide access to art in Salem for both residents and visitors.

“Chromed Dog”, Bikismo, Salem, Massachusetts, October 2017

Chromed Dog

There are three parts of the document. The first one is on the left side of the picture; we can see a light black streetlamp and brown buildings in the background. The second part is a mural in the middle of the photograph, which represents a chrome dog on a white environment. The third shows a three story-building on the right side. The photo is a middle-shot since we can’t really see the entire residential building and its surroundings. The lines on the mural are based on the letter “x”, starting from the dog’s left ear to its right paw and from its right ear to its left hind leg.
There are two elements that differ in this image: one featuring a beautiful artwork of a realistic cute dog, and the other depicting a plain building. It appears to be a low-income area since the balconies are small, and the overall look isn’t appealing.
We can only see the dog patiently waiting for something or someone. The dog is facing the viewer, sitting on its hind legs. It looks calm, with its attention seemingly focused on something surprising. It’s not an ordinary dog, it’s a chromed dog, as its relief and reflection are as realistic as if it was made of chrome, creating a stark contrast between the black and white parts of its body.
The photograph is a mid-shot image in three parts, featuring the mural “Chromed Dog”, painted by Bik Ismo in Salem, Massachusetts, in October 2017. Next to the artwork, there is a streetlamp facing the same direction as the chromed dog. On the right side, there’s a low-income residential building with small balconies, each made of different materials. In this mural, there’s a serious-looking grey chromed bulldog. Unlike the Mona Lisa painting, the dog doesn’t seem to be looking at us; its attention is focused outside of our view. There’s an ordinary streetlamp in front of it, at the level of its torso.
We can conclude that the mural was painted on the residential building to enhance its attractiveness. It arouses a sense of pride in the residents, allowing them to live with unique street art. Salem is one of the cities that contains the most diverse murals, attracting tourists and visitors. This spray paint masterpiece should be included in the Salem Punto Urban Art Museum, which is progressively gaining popularity among tourists and visitors.

What does he praise? The great show of art and love in favor of the neighborhood and the local community, and the inclusive world.
Art can exist in disadvantaged neighborhoods.
How does he manage to show it? (contrast, similarities, symbols, colors, lines…) The low contrast and similarities in colors between the mural and the urban surroundings.

What is the atmosphere like? Peaceful attitude of dog = peaceful area
Why did he choose that shot? Why not just the mural? To better showcase the realisation of art in the underprivileged community.
To stress the peaceful atmosphere and invite people to go to a peaceful neighborhood, to take away the negative prejudice visitors may have against the neighborhood.

What does the author want to say?

The author passes on a message to viewers that the mural was created with affection and volunteerism to bring happiness and pride to the neighborhood, transforming it into a colorful habitat where residents can feel joy instead of sadness while living in an underprivileged district.
The author surprises some observers by revealing that murals in Salem are more innocent and positive, rather than being considered offensive or vandalistic. These friendly and inspiring works are crafted by many talented street artists from all over the world who want to proudly express themselves in Salem, a city marked by witch trials in the 1690s and enduring discrimination. The author amuses those who believe that street art is always aggressive by showcasing “Chromed Dog”, a piece that perfectly captures an empathetic, realistic chrome dog. This surprises them and makes them appreciate it, even though it is a form of graffiti art.

What do I think about “Chromed Dog”?

This document is very interesting and cunning, especially for someone who used to believe that street art is always threatening. I like this demonstration; it is entertaining and efficient in explaining many things. I find it impressive to consider murals and graffiti as genuine art forms, given that they are created with great skill and effort, aiming to be as fascinating as possible. Personally, with time, street art has become the most efficient way to publicly share one’s expression and opinion in the realm of art. And I also appreciate that graffiti art goes beyond mere writing on the wall but evolves to include larger and more colorful works that become murals. In conclusion, I agree with the author.
I like this mural because the chrome dog is realistic and fascinating; the contrast between white and black is very clever. The bulldog has an interesting expression, because we can see that it waits quietly for something or someone. What's even more amusing is that the dog and the streetlamp seem to be facing in the same direction. The spotlight makes the grey dog appear more brilliant and courageous. I find the artist's decision to depict the reflection of the blue sky on the dog's body to emphasize the chrome effect very entertaining, making the work fantastic. The artist of “Chromed Dog” must be great in painting in the classical art style. Personally, I find the curious dog very cute and funny.
