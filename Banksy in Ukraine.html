Banksy published graffiti in Ukraine

One of the graffiti pieces in Ukraine

I choose the photograph of Banksy’s graffiti in Borodyanka, Ukraine, taken during the Russian invasion. The painting is sprayed on a destroyed building, in an area heavily impacted by Russian bombing raids. Cracks are visible, along with a brick foundation that is normally hidden, and another demolished building in the background. The sky in the top right corner appears gloomy and smoky. In the center, there are a boy and an adult wearing judo clothing. The one who has beaten is wearing a black ribbon on his torso, symbolizing a dangerous enemy. His face and hair resemble Vladimir Putin. This close-up picture focuses on the central figures, thereby making it difficult to see the surroundings, but effectively capturing the current situation.
The other judoka is a young but extraordinarily strong individual because he can deliver a knockout punch to his opponent. This can be interpreted as the young boy representing a small country being invaded by the menacing grown-man judoka depicting Russia. The strong child looks serious and furious since his eyes and forehead are dark. The unsuccessful judoka lies in a defeated pose, labeling him as a talentless warrior and diplomat. In the end, it appears that the child is winning over the unfairly dreadful tall adult.
I choose this photograph because I want to explain what Banksy’s graffiti wants to tell us and who the two characters are. I find this painting extremely intriguing and indisputable; there is no reason to talk otherwise about Russia’s terrible act, which goes against human right. Banksy encourages Ukraine for its right to exist and praises its tough defense against powerful war criminal, making his graffiti very popular. This spray paint isn’t the only in the Ukraine; Banksy has also painted many other artworks supporting Ukraine. I believe that his position also supports the Russian people who don’t agree with Putin’s decision to go to war against his neighbor.
Personally, this is the very emotional symbol of resistance against authoritarian, greedy Russia, which constantly want to steal territories without any exchange and compromise.
